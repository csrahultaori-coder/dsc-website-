Everything DSC holders in India need to know about the FIPS 140-2 to FIPS 140-3 cryptographic token transition — explained in plain language, kept up to date.

What is FIPS 140-3?

FIPS 140-3 is the current cryptographic module security standard published by NIST (US National Institute of Standards and Technology), used internationally — including by India's Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA) — to certify the hardware security tokens that store Digital Signature Certificate private keys. It succeeds FIPS 140-2 with updated, more rigorous testing requirements aligned with ISO/IEC 19790.

Impact on DSC Users

For everyday DSC holders — company directors, CAs, CS professionals, GST practitioners and tender contractors — this transition mainly affects the USB crypto token, not the certificate application process itself. As Certifying Authorities update their empanelled token list, new DSC issuances and renewals are gradually shifting to FIPS 140-3 certified tokens.

Token Migration — What You Should Know

  • Tokens already issued and working continue to function until certificate expiry, in most cases.
  • New DSC applications are increasingly issued only on FIPS 140-3 certified token models.
  • At renewal time, if your existing token is not on the currently approved list, a token replacement may be required alongside the certificate renewal.
  • We proactively check your token's certification status at every renewal so there are no last-minute surprises before a filing deadline.

Renewal Requirements Going Forward

When you approach us for a DSC renewal, we verify your current token model against the latest CCA-approved list. If it remains valid, we reissue your certificate on the same token at no extra hardware cost. If a replacement is needed, we supply a genuine FIPS 140-3 certified token and help migrate your signing setup with zero disruption to your filings.

Industry Updates

We track guidance from the CCA, licensed Certifying Authorities and token manufacturers as the FIPS 140-3 transition rolls out across India, and update this page as clearer timelines are officially communicated. For the most current status applicable to your specific certificate or token model, our team is the fastest way to get a definitive answer.

Quick Reference

FIPS 140-2 vs FIPS 140-3

FIPS 140-2 (Legacy)

The previous cryptographic module standard, widely used for existing DSC tokens issued over the past decade. Still functional for tokens already in circulation.

FIPS 140-3 (Current)

The updated global standard with stricter testing and validation requirements, now the default for new DSC token issuances as CAs complete their transition.

FAQs

FIPS 140-3 — Frequently Asked Questions

What is FIPS 140-3?
FIPS 140-3 (Federal Information Processing Standard Publication 140-3) is the latest US/international cryptographic module security standard, replacing the older FIPS 140-2. It defines security requirements for hardware and software cryptographic modules — including the USB tokens used to store your DSC private key.
Why does this affect my Digital Signature Certificate?
The Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA) mandates that DSC private keys be stored only on certified cryptographic hardware. As FIPS 140-2 certifications for older token models expire or are phased out, Certifying Authorities are moving to FIPS 140-3 certified tokens to remain compliant.
Will my existing FIPS 140-2 token stop working?
Existing tokens already issued generally continue to work until your certificate's validity ends. However, new issuances and most renewals are increasingly being issued only on FIPS 140-3 certified tokens as manufacturers and CAs transition. We recommend checking your specific token model with our team.
Do I need to buy a new token right now?
Not necessarily — if your current DSC and token are valid and working, there is no immediate need to replace it before expiry. However, if you are due for a renewal or a new DSC, we will issue it on a FIPS 140-3 certified token by default to future-proof your compliance.
Is there any extra cost for a FIPS 140-3 token?
Pricing is comparable to standard FIPS 140-2 tokens and is shared transparently before you proceed — there are no hidden charges for the upgraded certification.
How do I check if my token is FIPS 140-3 certified?
Send us your token's model number and manufacturer (usually printed on the token body or visible in the token management software) and we will confirm its certification status for you free of cost.
Does this apply to all DSC classes?
This primarily affects Class 3 DSC holders, which is the standard used for MCA, GST, Income Tax, e-Tendering and DGFT filings — the vast majority of business and professional users.
Where can I get an official update on this transition?
We track updates from the Controller of Certifying Authorities (CCA), NIST and licensed Certifying Authorities, and share practical guidance here as the transition progresses. For the latest applicability to your specific certificate, contact our team directly.
